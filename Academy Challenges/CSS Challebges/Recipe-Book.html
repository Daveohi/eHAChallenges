Jollof Rice – This easy, flavorful, and flawlessly cooked African classic dish Jollof rice is simple whether you choose to make it on the stovetop or take the easy way out by putting it all in the oven. With its well-seasoned goodness, including onion, garlic, and tomatoes with some nutritious veggies thrown in, it’s perfect for regular nights or special occasions! The soul-satisfying flavors from thyme, paprika, and a Scotch bonnet round out this dish to perfection.

Recipe #1

  • Time: 1hr 45min
  • Serves: Depends on how much of each ingredent you put in to the Rice
Ingredients Quantity
Rice As many cups as needed
Tomatoe Puree 1 Tin cup
Carrots and peas as many pieces
Onions 3 round balls
Thyme 1 pack
Paprika 1 sachet
Chicken Bouillon 1 Tin cup
Scotch bonnet pepper As many as needed
Chicken Broth As much as needed

Steps to making Jollof Rice

Step 1:Rinse the rice – Make sure you clean it thoroughly with just water. Do not parboil!

Step 2:Put It All In! Add all the ingredients to the baking dish or pan.

Step 3:Cover and Cook – Lastly, cover it with aluminum foil and pop it in the oven.

Recipe #2

Ogbono Soup is a super tasty, nutrient-dense, and hearty Nigerian soup made with ogbono seeds (African wild mango seeds), classic West African spices, and your choice of meat and fish. It’s reminiscent of okra stew and goes so well with tasty, filling pounded yams for an extraordinary African meal experience!

Ingredients Quantity
Meat As many cups as needed
Smoked Fish As many as needed
Ogbono seeds 3 Tin cups
Red Palm Oil 1/3 cup
Crayfish As much as needed
Chicken Bouillon 1 Tin cup
Green As much as needed

Steps to making ogbono soup

Step 1:Cook the Meat – In a medium-sized saucepan, boil meat and season with salt and pepper until tender (approximately 30-60 minutes depending on the choice of meat). You can shorten this process in half with a pressure cooker.

Step 2:Cook Tougher Cuts Separately – Boil the cow skin and tripe together or separately until tender; use your best judgment. Remove cow skin tripe and add to the pot of boiled meat. Then add the smoked fish to the pot if using it.

Step 3:Reserve enough stock from the meat (about 3-4 cups).

Step 4:Ogbono Paste – Mix ground ogbono with red palm oil, then add to the pot of boiled meat, followed by crayfish and scotch bonnet pepper. Bring to a boil. Then simmer it for 10 minutes or more. Add chicken bouillon or cubes if desired.

Step 4:Adjust Texture and Add Greens – Add more stock or water for the desired thickness. Then add spinach (or other greens), cook for 2-3 minutes more, turn the stove off, and serve piping hot.
